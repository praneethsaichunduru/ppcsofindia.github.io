Chennai General Post Office

சென்னை பொது அஞ்சல் அலுவலகம்

The Chennai General Post Office (GPO), located on Rajaji Salai at Parry's Corner, stands as a testament to the city's rich colonial heritage. Designed by the British architect Robert Chisholm, the building was inaugurated on April 26, 1884, after a decade of construction that commenced in 1874. The project was completed at a cost of ₹6.8 lakh. The GPO was established to centralize and enhance postal services in the region, reflecting the administrative advancements of that era.

Architecturally, the Chennai GPO is a magnificent example of the Indo-Saracenic style, a fusion of Indian and Gothic elements. The three-storied structure spans 352 feet in length and 162 feet in width, encompassing a floor area of approximately 55,000 square feet. Its most striking features are the 125-foot-tall twin towers that once boasted Kerala-style caps, which were unfortunately lost to a mid-20th-century storm. The building's design includes a high-ceilinged central hall, with the ground floor originally housing stores and service areas, the first floor designated for offices, and the second floor providing accommodation for officers.

Over the years, the building has faced challenges, including a significant fire in October 2000 that caused extensive damage to about 15,000 square feet of its area. Following this, a major restoration project was undertaken, culminating in the reopening of the first two floors in November 2005. In 2011, a portion of the roof collapsed due to heavy monsoon rains, highlighting the ongoing need for maintenance of this historic structure.

Recognizing its historical and architectural significance, the Department of Posts has initiated further restoration efforts. In November 2023, a Memorandum of Understanding was signed with the Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH) to restore the 150-year-old building. INTACH's expertise in renovating heritage structures is expected to play a crucial role in preserving the GPO's legacy for future generations.
