Adi Yogi

ஆதியோகி

The Adiyogi statue, a monumental 112-foot-tall sculpture, stands majestically at the Isha Yoga Center near Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. Unveiled on Mahashivaratri in 2017 by India's Prime Minister, Narendra Modi, this statue holds the Guinness World Record for the largest bust sculpture. It represents Adiyogi, the first yogi, who, over 15,000 years ago, transmitted the science of yoga to his seven disciples, the Saptarishis. The height of 112 feet symbolizes the 112 methods he offered for human beings to transcend their limitations and reach their ultimate potential. The statue serves as a source of inspiration, emphasizing that "in is the only way out" for human well-being and liberation.

Adjacent to the Adiyogi statue is the Yogeshwar Linga, a consecrated space that enhances the spiritual ambiance of the area. Visitors can engage in various offerings and practices here, such as making offerings of water and neem leaves or participating in the Adiyogi Pradakshina, a circumambulation that fosters receptivity to Adiyogi's grace. The site is open daily from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM, welcoming seekers from around the globe.

The Isha Foundation, established by Sadhguru Jaggi Vasudev, is the organization behind the creation of the Adiyogi statue. Founded with the mission to elevate human consciousness and promote global harmony through individual transformation, the foundation offers a plethora of programs rooted in the ancient science of yoga. These programs aim to enhance physical, mental, and spiritual well-being, guiding participants toward a deeper understanding of life and realization of their fullest potential.

Beyond its spiritual initiatives, Isha Foundation is deeply committed to social outreach and environmental sustainability. One of its flagship projects, Project GreenHands, is an environmental movement aiming to increase the green cover of Tamil Nadu by planting trees and restoring soil health. Another significant initiative, Action for Rural Rejuvenation, seeks to transform the lives of rural populations by providing essential services and fostering community development. In the educational realm, Isha Vidhya focuses on offering quality education to rural children, ensuring they have access to opportunities that can uplift their communities.

The impact of Isha Foundation's initiatives is profound, touching the lives of millions worldwide. With the support of over 17 million volunteers across 300 centers globally, the foundation's activities address various facets of human well-being. From offering inner transformation programs to spearheading environmental and educational projects, Isha Foundation exemplifies a holistic approach to personal and societal betterment. The Adiyogi statue stands as a testament to this vision, symbolizing the potential for individual transformation and the timeless relevance of yogic science.
