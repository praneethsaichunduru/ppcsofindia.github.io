Buddha Statue

బుద్ధ విగ్రహం

The Buddha statue in Hussain Sagar, Hyderabad, is a significant landmark that symbolizes peace and cultural heritage. Hussain Sagar, an artificial lake built in 1562 by Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah, was originally constructed to serve as a water reservoir for Hyderabad and Secunderabad. Designed by engineer Hussain Shah Wali, the lake eventually evolved into a major tourist attraction with boating, ferry rides, and scenic views along its shores. The Tank Bund road and Necklace Road, running along its perimeter, enhance its appeal, making it one of the city’s most visited locations. Over the years, the lake has become not just a scenic spot but also a hub of historical and cultural significance.

The idea of installing a massive Buddha statue in the middle of the lake was conceived by former Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (NTR) in the late 1980s. Inspired by global monuments like the Statue of Liberty, he envisioned a grand structure to promote peace and harmony. The statue, sculpted from a single granite rock sourced from Raigiri near Hyderabad, stands at 18 meters tall and weighs around 350 tons, making it the world's tallest monolithic Buddha statue in a standing posture. Over 200 artisans, led by renowned sculptor Ganapathi Sthapathi, meticulously carved the statue over two years before it was transported for installation in the lake.

A major tragedy struck in 1990 during the transportation of the statue. As it was being ferried to its designated platform in the middle of Hussain Sagar, the barge carrying it capsized, causing the statue to sink into the waters. This accident led to the unfortunate loss of ten workers’ lives. The statue remained submerged for nearly two years before an extensive and challenging recovery effort was undertaken. With significant engineering expertise and determination, the statue was finally retrieved and successfully installed on a specially constructed platform known as the "Gibraltar Rock" in 1992. Following its installation, the Dalai Lama consecrated the statue, further enhancing its spiritual significance.

Today, the Buddha statue and Hussain Sagar together form an integral part of Hyderabad’s cultural and recreational landscape. The lake is surrounded by various attractions, including Lumbini Park, from where visitors can take boat rides to the statue. The area features laser and musical fountain shows, and the Tank Bund road is lined with statues of notable Telugu figures, adding historical value to the surroundings. The statue holds special importance during Buddha Purnima when it is illuminated and decorated, attracting thousands of devotees and tourists. The lake also serves as a center for water sports, including sailing and speedboat rides, making it a dynamic and vibrant location throughout the year.

Despite its beauty and significance, Hussain Sagar has faced environmental concerns due to pollution from industrial and domestic waste. Several initiatives have been launched to clean the lake and restore its ecological balance, emphasizing sustainability and conservation. However, the Buddha statue continues to stand as a symbol of peace, resilience, and spiritual wisdom. It remains a monumental tribute to the teachings of Gautama Buddha, promoting harmony and non-violence in the heart of Hyderabad, making it not just a tourist attraction but also a beacon of tranquility in the bustling city.
